Telescopes

Refracting Telescopes

Also known as "dioptric telescopes," these telescopes use lenses to bend and focus light to create an image. Examples include the classic Galilean and Keplerian telescopes, and the modern Meade Infinity 102mm Altazimuth Refractor.

Reflecting Telescopes

Also known as "catoptric telescopes," these telescopes use mirrors to collect and focus light to create an image. Examples include the Newtonian telescope and the Ritchey-Chretien telescope.

Cassegrain Telescopes

These telescopes use a combination of mirrors and lenses to create an image. Examples include the Schmidt-Cassegrain telescope and the Maksutov-Cassegrain telescope.

Dobsonian Telescopes

These are a type of reflecting telescope that is mounted on a simple, sturdy base. They are often quite large and offer a lot of light-gathering power for their price. Examples include the Sky-Watcher Dobsonian Telescope and the Orion SkyQuest XT8 Classic Dobsonian Telescope.

Catadioptric Telescopes

These telescopes use both mirrors and lenses to create an image. Examples include the Schmidt-Cassegrain telescope, the Maksutov-Cassegrain telescope, and the Schmidt-Newtonian telescope.

Radio Telescopes

These telescopes use a dish or array of dishes to collect and focus radio waves emitted by celestial objects. Examples include the Very Large Array and the Atacama Large Millimeter Array (ALMA).

Space Telescopes

These telescopes are placed in orbit around the Earth or beyond to observe the Universe without the interference of the Earth's atmosphere. Examples include the Hubble Space Telescope, the Chandra X-ray Observatory, and the James Webb Space Telescope
